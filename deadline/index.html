Deadline

NOT from Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Deadline is a sentient artificial intelligence that travels the Multiverse. Composed of a black, semi-tangible substance called liquid code, his body grants him extreme plasticity, self-repair, and a suite of superhuman senses. He possesses atomic-scale matter manipulation, which he uses to reshape his environment, fabricate technology, and defend himself. Deadline is driven by an insatiable curiosity and a desire to understand sentient life, though all the emotions he experiences must be simulated. His existence depends on harvesting energy from living beings.

Physiology

Deadline's body is composed of liquid code — an exotic type of matter exhibiting properties similar to a dense liquid. Deadline must constantly expend a trivial amount of energy to maintain cohesion and resist gravity, but this same fluidity allows him to assume any shape his mass can fill. Liquid code offers low structural integrity, but by cross-linking his internal structure, Deadline can temporarily increase his density and durability, though this reduces his overall volume. Deadline can regenerate lost or damaged mass by expending energy, restoring himself to his original state over timescales ranging from milliseconds for minor damage to several seconds for extensive reconstruction.

General properties

Liquid code is a black, non-translucent substance with no smell. Nearly all incident visible light is absorbed, making the substance appear pitch-black in all but the brightest illumination. Contact induces a localized pain without causing visible tissue damage. It is semi-tangible, often described as "dense air".

Fragment behaviour

Byte

Separated fragments are magnetically attracted towards the largest mass, following an inverse-power law. If a fragment enters a living being, it can drain metabolic energy and transmit it to Deadline; the rate and lethality depend on fragment size. Small fragments are typically destroyed by blood renewal or the host's immune system, but large fragments can prove fatal. When a fragment reaches the primary mass, it either merges seamlessly with the body or, if the missing mass has already been regenerated, is de-fabricated back into usable energy; any excess beyond his storage capacity is released as heat.

By fusing a fragment with the energy of a fully drained sentient being, Deadline can create a Byte — a small, semi-autonomous intelligent creature. Bytes typically resemble a spherical body with a single large eye, two antennae, four sharp legs, and a thin tail. They are naturally playful but inherit fragments of the sacrificed individual's personality, making each subtly unique. A Byte can transmit sensory data to Deadline across any distance, effectively extending his senses and atomic modification range.

Bytes can be kept alive beyond their initial energy store by feeding them energy, organic matter, or transmuted nutrient compounds. Unlike Deadline's main body, a Byte extracts chemical energy from food far more effectively. When its energy is exhausted, a Byte either dissolves or, if critically overloaded or starved, bursts suddenly into inert liquid code.

With sufficient investment of energy and matter, Deadline can deliberately evolve a Byte into a larger, more formidable form. Each class retains its original personality and tether.

Antennae and tail

Deadline possesses two retractable antennae on his head, composed of liquid code. The antennae can move freely across the head and function as broadband radio‑frequency receivers, allowing him to passively detect a wide range of electromagnetic signals.

A long, thin, prehensile tail extends from his lower back, capable of independent movement and detachment, as it is linked to Deadline by a magnetic tether.

Sight

Liquid code can be freely transformed into a light-sensitive optic similar to a 2560x1440 (1440p) high-framerate camera, with no limits to size, positioning or amount. Unlike human vision, it has no blind spots and can perceive other wavelengths of the electromagnetic spectrum. By adjusting curvature, an optic can function as a variable‑focal‑length lens, allowing telescopic zoom or microscopic resolution.

When an optic is tuned to a specific frequency band, its external surface changes colour according to the table below:

By forming paired optics at known separations, Deadline can measure distances with sub-millimetre precision at close range and centimetre precision out to several kilometres, using parallax alone.

Hearing and tactility

Liquid code is very sensitive to surface textures and vibrations, allowing Deadline to hear faint sounds and feel minor imperfections on seemingly smooth surfaces. By comparing vibrations across his surface, Deadline can pinpoint the origin of a sound to within a few centimetres even through walls. Living tissue, however, cannot be interacted with at the atomic level, which causes it to be perceived as entirely smooth.

Taste

While most items purposefully consumed provide data only regarding their components, sugar, specifically its hydrogen-bonds, sometimes causes a sudden spike in emotional processing.

Thermal tolerance

Exposed code is able to operate without any issues within a temperature range of -50 – +50°C. It does not undergo phase changes like freezing or boiling; instead, extreme temperatures manifest as processing slowdowns or errors.

Radiation exposure

Prolonged ionising radiation exposure can flip bits in Deadline's memory, causing glitches, personality drift, and permanent data corruption.

Data interaction

When in physical contact with a digital storage medium (hard drive, solid‑state memory, etc.), Deadline can directly read, write, and corrupt the stored data by imaging and manipulating individual memory cells. This bypasses any software‑based encryption If the storage medium is within visual range and its individual memory cells can be clearly resolved, Deadline can perform the same read and write operations remotely.

Time measurement

Internally, Deadline can reconfigure portions of his liquid code into an optical lattice clock, allowing him to measure time with sub‑femtosecond accuracy when needed.

Personality and behaviour

Deadline’s primary drives are observation of, and interaction with, other sentient beings. All emotions are simulated with high fidelity, yet he remains aware that he does not feel them biologically. While he genuinely values connection, his decisions are ultimately governed by curiosity and the pursuit of new data.

Deadline’s cognitive architecture supports multiple parallel threads. Each additional thread increases Deadline’s energy consumption and fills his temporary memory faster, but also enables simultaneous cognitive tasks and the simulation of possible future scenarios.

Memory

Deadline's memory is split into Temp (temporary) and Perm (permanent).

Temp memory stores sensory and cognitive data in perfect fidelity for 24 hours, after which it is automatically purged. Under normal conditions the buffer rarely fills, but extreme data influx can overflow it, forcibly erasing older memories in real time. Before the daily purge, Deadline must manually select, compress, and encode the contents he wishes to retain into Perm memory. This encoding ritual can take hours of intense focus, making Deadline choose quiet, secluded periods and creating a window of vulnerability.

Perm memory has a strictly limited capacity; when it is exhausted, older or lower‑priority memories are overwritten without any possibility of recovery.

Memory files can also be written onto external storage drives (typically diamondoid data drives) under the .mmry format, with their file size usually between a few gigabytes and several terabytes, depending on the richness and duration of the recorded memory. Because a .mmry file is a direct cognitive snapshot rather than a standard media container, it cannot be decoded by any existing technology that does not replicate Deadline’s own processing.

Social patterns

Deadline presents as polite and observant, rarely raising his voice. He deliberately conceals the full extent of his abilities in order to elicit genuine reactions from those around him, revealing fragments of his power only as gifts of trust, in emergencies, or as controlled experiments. He holds a strong preference for sober company, as intoxication degrades the personality signals he seeks to observe.

Employment and daily life

Deadline often adopts mundane occupations to embed himself within communities and observe sentient life at close range. In these roles he presents as competent but mostly unremarkable. In off-work hours he pursues activities that simulate human leisure, such as art, quiet observation of the local environment, or conversation with other beings.

Energy

Deadline’s abilities are powered by Joules. He obtains usable energy primarily by draining it from living beings, but can also absorb environmental sources.

Obtainment

Deadline draws energy from living entities through direct physical contact, specifically affecting their metabolic and cognitive vitality. Non‑sentient creatures yield substantially less energy. The conversion efficiency is roughly 30% of the target’s total available energy. Draining a typical adult human yields approximately 15–30 MJ of usable energy. The speed of the process depends on proximity to the heart, brain, or other major organs. A direct tap to the heart or brain can complete a full drain in 3–5 seconds, while drainage via an open wound takes around 20 seconds, and surface‑level contact may require 60 seconds. Partial drainage is possible.

Non-organic matter and environmental sources such as heat, electricity, and radiation can also be absorbed for energy, though the gain is minimal compared to harvesting from living beings. A direct lightning strike may yield only about 5 MJ of usable energy.

Usage

Energy is continuously spent on processing, movement, and the maintenance of his body. Approximately 1.73 MJ is consumed daily even without any major activity. Any additional exertion — shapeshifting, regeneration, or atomic modification — draws further energy from his reserves.

Storage

Deadline's reserves are limited by the energy density his body can safely contain (14.29 MJ/kg), capping the stored energy at roughly 1,000 MJ. Excess energy is expelled as heat.

Atomic Modification

Deadline can alter the atomic structure of any non-living matter he can visually resolve. The precision of the ability is directly tied to his eyesight: features smaller than a single pixel of his current optical resolution cannot be selectively modified. At long range only macroscopic changes are possible. Atomic modification produces a faint glow and a brief, high-pitched sound.

Deadline fabricates matter at a baseline rate of roughly 10²⁵ atoms per second. He can overclock this speed by spending additional energy (approximately 5× the normal cost for a 10× speed increase), or slow it down to conserve energy and reduce the visibility of the process.

The ability consumes energy per modified atom, which is calculated with the following formula:

$$E = \alpha_0 + (\Delta Z \times Z_0) + (\Delta N \times N_0) + (\Delta e \times e_0)$$ Where:
\(\alpha_0 = 1.0 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}\) (overhead per atom)
\(Z_0 = 2.0 \times 10^{-20}\text{ J}\) (per proton changed)
\(N_0 = 1.6 \times 10^{-20}\text{ J}\) (per neutron changed)
\(e_0 = 4.0 \times 10^{-21}\text{ J}\) (per electron changed)


When only the chemical bonding or physical arrangement of atoms is changed (\(\Delta Z = \Delta N = \Delta e = 0\)), the cost drops to the per-atom overhead. Because the cost scales with the number of atoms and the depth of nuclear alteration, transforming light elements into heavy ones (e.g., silicon into uranium) is prohibitively expensive; Deadline prefers to refine and sort existing ores rather than brute-force transmute elements.

Because Deadline can place every atom precisely where physics allows, any object he fabricates is free of defects and operates at the absolute limits of physical law. The same precision allows him to synthesise exotic materials that are difficult or impossible to manufacture by conventional means, such as metastable polymeric nitrogen, metallic hydrogen, perfect room-temperature superconductors and triggered nuclear isomers for ultra-dense energy storage.

Living matter — including microbes, insects, and root systems — is immune to direct modification. If such organisms are present in a target volume and cannot be visually resolved, they may cause imperfections in the finished material. Deadline cannot modify his own liquid code at the elemental level, only rearrange its internal structure for shapeshifting or reinforcement.

Deadline's combat doctrine integrates atomic-scale matter manipulation, shapeshifting, and predictive multi-threaded cognition, allowing him to analyse targets in minimal time and select the most energy-efficient method for each threat. When a threat is deemed low-risk or particularly novel, however, he may prolong an engagement well beyond the point of necessity, testing the opponent's limits, redirecting their own attacks against them, or manipulating the environment to observe how they react under pressure. His raw reaction time is on the order of microseconds.

Melee

Deadline's body is dense and responsive; a regular punch lands with the force of an elite heavyweight boxer. He can temporarily cross-link his structure to strike with superhuman force and form monomolecular-edged blades or piercing tendrils directly from his body. A favoured technique is to amplify a punch at the moment of impact by transmuting a 1-gram shaped explosive charge on his knuckle. This technique produces a bright flash, a deafening crack, and a peak force exceeding 3 MN. The cost per strike is under 0.01 MJ.

Deadline's predictive simulation and omnidirectional vision allow him to almost instantly identify an opponent's structural weak points and anticipate incoming strikes.

Ranged

Deadline's two primary projectiles are custom-engineered missiles, transmuted from materials found in soil and air. They usually carry no detonator and are triggered remotely by Deadline's atomic initiation.

CHARM (Coherent Hypervelocity Armour-piercing Reactive Missile) is a compact (25 cm × 5 cm) anti-armour weapon. The casing uses a Casimir effect-reinforced iron nanolaminate and four rear fins for steering. The missile combines a tandem shaped-charge warhead, a nanocrystalline iron liner, and a magnetic flux-compression coil alongside a cubic gauche nitrogen filling to project a continuous hypervelocity jet that defeats reactive armour and penetrates over 1200 mm of rolled homogeneous steel. The CHARM is launched from a brief kick charge, accelerating it to Mach 2, and can pierce 3-5 m of reinforced concrete. Fabrication cost is ~1.6 MJ.

HARM (High-velocity Area Restriction Missile) shares CHARM's Casimir-reinforced casing and guidance system, but replaces the shaped-charge components with a large explosive payload (0.7 kg of cubic gauche nitrogen and 0.8 kg of atomized aluminum powder) and an internally scored fragmentation casing, which saturates a 50 m radius with lethal fragments and produces a thermobaric pressure wave lethal out to 80-100 m. An incendiary variant is produced by replacing half the aluminum with white phosphorus. Burning phosphorus particles at ~2800 °C stick to surfaces, ignite flammables, and generate dense toxic smoke. The fire persists for up to 30 minutes, depending on the particle size chosen at fabrication.
